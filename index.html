Meeting Schedules

Crimson Defense meets every Wednesday at 6pm in The Tom Bevill Building Room 0009.. All meeting topics will be posted on our Discord which is linked below and sent to the mailing list. You can also manually check the schedule on our calendar.

Some previous topics we have studied include denial of service attacks, malware analysis, nmap scans, SQL injection, phishing, and cryptography. We also have professional development meetings where representitives from companies in both the public and private sectors come to share their experiences, their work culture, and speak on any upcoming internships and job openings.

Every 6pm on Tuesdays our competition team meets in The Tom Bevill Building Room 0009! Each semester, we compete in the National Cyber League CTF as well as at least one other kind of challenge. Our competition team meetings aim to sharpen your cyber security skills to participate in these competitions, no experience is necessary to join the competition team and for more information please refer to the #competitions-info channel in our discord.

Project Crimson: The University of Alabama's Cyber Security Mentorship Program - brought to you by The Crimson Defense - Cyber Security Club meets every Wednesday at 5pm - 5:45pm CDT right before general club in the Cyber Lab (Tom Bevill Building Room 0009). Project Crimson aims to teach real world cyber security techniques in a one on one form. Project Crimson is student led, where all of our mentors are cyber security students at The University of Alabama.

Mentors are host workshops where they give immediate help to any student that comes in during this time on a wide variety of topics relating to cyber security, we reccomend attending this program if you want immediate help with cyber security topics or want a more in depth learning on different programs and technologies!

Mentors are also taught soft skills through the means of smaller leadership workshops held at the end of each and every meeting these topics can include, teamwork, goal setting, communication workshops, and lesson organization workshops.

Any questions about attending Project Crimson or becoming a Project Crimson mentor should be directed to Project Crimson Directors Grace Neil and Light Ruder.

Missions

  • Teach real, applicable security skills (penetration testing, malware analysis, etc.)

  • Advocate for cyber security curriculum at the University of Alabama. Our work in this area has helped to create the new College of Engineering Cyber Security Major and Computer Science Concentration in Cyber Security at UA!

  • Compete in Cyber Security Competitions at the local, state, and national level. We typically participate in the NCL CTF and one other different competition each semester.
